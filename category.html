Suitable sports glasses for long training sessions

Whether you are running, triathlon, hiking or cycling - a comfortable fit is a must, even during long and intensive training sessions. That's why you should make sure that your sports glasses have an adjustable nose piece and rubberised temples. This ensures that the glasses stay exactly where they are supposed to be, even during a sweaty hike, and prevents annoying slipping. Our multifunctional sports glasses The HAWK and The FALCON also have a magnetic sweatband. This keeps your eyes dry even during strenuous training sessions. In addition, a retainer can be attached to the ends of the temples on The HAWK and The FALCON can be attached. This ensures a perfect hold twice over.

Sporty all-rounder: Glasses for versatile use

High-quality sports glasses are also characterised by many functions and a wide range of uses. Once you have found the right model, you can use sports glasses for many outdoor activities. You don't have to buy separate road cycling glasses, running glasses, triathlon glasses or cross-country skiing glasses.

The right lens for sports glasses: What should you look out for?

The most important function of sports sunglasses is of course the protection of the eyes. 100% UV400 protection is indispensable. You can read more about UV rays here. In addition to UV protection, the tint of the sports glasses is also an important criterion when choosing. Lenses have different anti-glare categories. Basically, we can choose between lenses with category 0 to category 4 (CAT. 0 to CAT. 4). Depending on the category, different amounts of light come through the lens. Depending on the light conditions or the weather conditions, you can find the right lens . Lenses with category 0 (CAT. 0) are transparent and especially recommended for very dark and foggy conditions or when trail running through the forest. Lenses with filter category 1 (CAT. 1) are also suitable for low light and poorer conditions. Category 2 and 3 (CAT. 2 and CAT. 3), on the other hand, are good all-round lenses. sports glasses with CAT. 2 and CAT. 3 lenses are perfect for normal to fair conditions. If you are mainly out and about in sunny conditions, then sports glasses with light category 4 (CAT. 4) are the ones to go for. Want to know more about filter categories and the VLT value? You can find out more here.

Sports glasses with interchangeable lenses: Prepared for all eventualities

Anyone who likes to be out in nature knows how quickly the weather can change and how often the lighting conditions can change. sports glasses with interchangeable lenses are the solution to this problem. Thanks to the simple exchange system, the lenses of the multifunctional glasses The HAWK and The FALCON can be exchanged within a few seconds. This means you are perfectly equipped for all weather conditions.

Polarised sports glasses: What are the advantages and disadvantages?

For many outdoor athletes, it is important that their sports glasses are equipped with a polarising filter. But what does polarisation actually mean? The filter minimises annoying reflections and enhances contrasts. Since you are exposed to many reflections, especially in nature or on the water, many athletes opt for polarised sports glasses. One disadvantage of polarised lenses, however, is that they are harder to read from smartphones, sports watches, bicycle computer displays or car dashboards. You can read more advantages and disadvantages of sports glasses with polarised lenses in the polarising filter guide.

Self-tinting (photochromatic) sports glasses for men and women

Have you ever heard of photochromic cycling glasses? These are sports glasses, which have self-tinting lenses. This is very practical, especially in changing light conditions, as you are equipped with sports glasses for different weather conditions. The lenses change from category 1 (CAT. 1) to category 3 (CAT. 3) and automatically get lighter and darker during sports. Photochromic sports glasses of course also offer 100% UV400 protection and block harmful UVA and UVB rays.
